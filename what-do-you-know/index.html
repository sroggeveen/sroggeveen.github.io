My Family

I consider my family a huge part of my identity; both my family in Korea and my family in the US are very important to me.
My family in the US is predominately on my dad's side, they live in Boston, MA. I see my aunt, uncle, and cousins almost every year, and I am very close with my cousins.
Last summer, I visited Korea for the first time and I got to connect with that aspect of my identity. I met my imos (aunts), samchuns (uncles), and cousins for the first time. These were really special moments for me.
And of course, my immediate family consists of my mom, dad, and sister, whom I hold very close to me.

My Friends

I consider my friends to be my chosen family, so I hold them very close to me also. Whether we met inside or out of school, my friends (not all pictured) are consistently a large part of my identity and influence who I am and how I act. Without my wonderful friends, I would not be the person I am today!
As corny as this sounds, I deeply value the people whom I have chosen to surround myself with, and I couldn't ask for a better chosen family!
